Exams vs ProjectsA Debate on the Future of Education

🏛️ SCHOOL DEBATE · 2026

📜 MOTION

"Schools should cancel all written exams
and replace them with practical projects."

✅ FOR · Proposition

Cancel Exams. Embrace Projects.

  • Real-world skills: projects teach collaboration, research, and problem-solving that exams never test
  • Less anxiety: countless students suffer from test stress; projects are naturally paced
  • Deeper learning: building something forces understanding; memorizing for a test is forgotten in a week
  • Fairness: not every student performs well under timed pressure; projects level the playing field
  • Future-ready: universities and employers value portfolios over report cards
❌ AGAINST · Opposition

Keep Exams. Reform Them.

  • Objective measurement: exams provide a standardized way to compare knowledge across schools
  • Accountability: without exams, how do we ensure every student actually masters the basics?
  • Time & resources: grading 50 science fair projects fairly is far harder than marking one test
  • Preparation for reality: university entrance exams, professional certifications, and job interviews are all tests
  • Projects have bias: teachers may favor creative students; exams are blind to personality
VS

⚡ The Real Question

Can we find a middle ground — keep exams for accountability but add projects for depth?

🔑 KEY CLASH POINTS

1

Fairness: Are timed exams really fair to all learners, or do they favor fast thinkers?

2

Quality: Does a project grade truly reflect knowledge better than an exam score?

3

Feasibility: Can schools with 40+ students per class realistically assess projects?

4

Cheating: Who did the work — the student, the parent, or ChatGPT?

5

Balance: Should we replace all exams, or could a 50/50 hybrid work better?

6

Future: In an AI age, does memorizing facts still matter, or is doing more important?
